Birkeland-Eyde Reactor

This project page introduces the historical idea of fixing nitrogen from air using electrical energy. Keep this as a theory, history, and design-review topic unless you have qualified supervision and professional safety controls.

Learn

  • Why nitrogen fixation matters
  • How plasma changes reaction conditions
  • Why gas handling and ventilation are critical

Project Display

  • Reference photo
  • Non-operational diagram
  • Safety review checklist

Step-by-step safe guide

  1. Write the project question: how did electric arcs help fix nitrogen from air?
  2. Research the historical process and label the air flow, arc chamber, cooling, and gas-cleanup stages in a diagram.
  3. Build a non-powered display model with cardboard, clear tubing, and wire to show the layout without electricity or gases.
  4. Add a safety card listing the real hazards: high voltage, hot plasma, nitrogen oxides, ventilation, and corrosion.
  5. Photograph the model, then explain what professional controls a real laboratory version would need.
  6. Finish with one improvement idea, such as a clearer diagram, better labels, or a short video explanation.

Public AV Science guide: do not build or operate an electric arc reactor at home.

Ammonia Synthesis

A research-style overview of how ammonia is made industrially and why pressure, catalysts, and temperature change the outcome. This page should not become a home synthesis recipe.

Learn

  • Reaction equilibrium
  • Industrial catalyst concepts
  • Why gases and pressure need expert controls

Safe Direction

  • Add a history timeline
  • Compare industrial process diagrams
  • Include a glossary of key terms

Step-by-step safe guide

  1. Draw a Haber-Bosch process loop showing nitrogen, hydrogen, reactor, separator, recycle, and product output.
  2. Make a terms panel for catalyst, equilibrium, pressure, temperature, and yield.
  3. Use colored beads or paper arrows to model molecules entering, reacting, separating, and recycling.
  4. Add a timeline showing why ammonia synthesis changed farming and industry.
  5. Compare traditional ammonia production with green-ammonia ideas such as renewable hydrogen.
  6. Record a short conclusion explaining why real ammonia synthesis requires industrial equipment.

Public AV Science guide: this is a research/modeling project, not a home ammonia synthesis recipe.

Potassium Nitrate Concepts

A safer project page can teach solubility, crystallization, filtration, and responsible chemical storage without giving unsafe instructions for making or using oxidizers.

Learn

  • Solubility and temperature
  • Crystal formation
  • Filtration and purity

Safer Alternative

  • Grow table-salt crystals
  • Compare sugar crystal shapes
  • Photograph crystal growth over time

Step-by-step safer crystal guide

  1. Choose a safer crystal material such as table salt, sugar, or alum instead of making oxidizers.
  2. With adult supervision, dissolve the safe material in warm water until no more dissolves.
  3. Filter the liquid through a coffee filter so dust or undissolved grains stay out.
  4. Pour the clear solution into a labeled jar and leave it undisturbed as it cools and evaporates.
  5. Photograph the crystals each day and record size, shape, and growth speed.
  6. Compare the safe crystals with potassium nitrate as a research topic, including storage and hazard differences.

Public AV Science guide: do not make, concentrate, store, or test nitrate oxidizers at home.

Induction Heater

This page frames the induction heater as an electronics design and safety-planning project. It should include power limits, insulation, heat risk, and supervision before any build notes.

Learn

  • Magnetic fields and eddy currents
  • Switching circuits and coils
  • Thermal and electrical safety

Project Display

  • Parts reference image
  • Risk checklist
  • Low-voltage starter demo

Step-by-step safe build plan

  1. Start with the question: how can a changing magnetic field heat metal without a flame?
  2. Draw a block diagram with power source, driver circuit, work coil, test object, cooling, and emergency stop.
  3. Build an unpowered coil model first so you can photograph and explain the layout safely.
  4. Use a circuit simulator or a certified low-voltage training kit instead of improvised mains-powered hardware.
  5. If a supervised kit is used, follow the manufacturer manual, keep flammable items away, and stop if wires or parts overheat.
  6. Document voltage rating, current limit, temperature observations, and one design improvement for safer testing.

Public AV Science guide: do not build high-current or mains-powered induction heaters without qualified supervision.
